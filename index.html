Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Correlations between Poverty % and Health Risks
The median age of the population studied across 51 states range from 30.5 years to 44.1 years. Studying the effects of Poverty % to the lack of healthcare , smoking % and the obesity % we infer the following:
  • There is a positive correlation between the poverty percentage and lack of healthcare. The calculated correlation coefficient is 0.52
  • There is a positive correlation between poverty percentage and number of smokers. The calcualted correlation coefficient is 0.52
  • There is a positive correlation between poverty percentage and Obesity percentage. The calculated correlation coefficient is 0.46
Correlations between Age and Health Risks
The median age of the population studied across 51 states range from 30.5 years to 44.1 years. Studying the effects of median age to the lack of healthcare , smoking % and the obesity % we infer the following,but since we have the median age the results may not be accurate and may be skewed.
  • There is a negative correlation between the Median Age and lack of healthcare. As people age the number of people having healthcare coverage increases. The calculated correlation coefficient is -0.38522
  • There is a weak positive correlation between age and number of smokers.The calculated correlation coefficient is 0.248
  • There is a negative correlation between age and Obesity percentage.The calcualted correlation coefficient is -0.00769.
